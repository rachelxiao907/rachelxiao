5/14: AP Chemistry

This AP was very stressful as the first question was 25 minutes and the second question with 15 minutes, each with five minutes to submit. I took the test in my dining room because it had the best lighting, wifi, and space for the exam. As a result, no one went to the first floor during my exam at 2:00-2:50. I used Victoria's school iPad to take the test because it was large enough for me to read the questions easily and it could take pictures.

During the exam: I had to log in 30 minutes before my exam was administrated and the wait was nerve-racking. I watched the timer slowly tick down as I lost my mind while watching tiktoks. The test was nothing like the practice... I felt rushed as the first question went up to K and the second question went up to H. The questions were basic yet complex and my test submitted smoothly and clearly.
After the exam: I was so relieved that it was over and all of my friends who took the chemistry exam went on Houseparty and discussed the difficulty. Some of my friends' questions went up to the letter L! Overall, we were relieved and thought it wasn't completely terrible.

6/1 - 6/12: Intro CS Project

I dedicated a lot of time to my CS project that was rather straight foward, yet ambiguous at the same time. I spent a lot of time calling my friends, especially Amanda and Jessica. They kept me motivated throughout the entire project, and I don't think I would've gotten a 95 on the project without them.

The goal of the project was to create a NetLogo simulation that represented a real world problem that could be tested. It was hard to find a topic, but I ended up creating a simulation about how wind speeds affect pollen. This project was going smoothly until June 10. I saw Daniela's project and it was crazy. It was a great simulation with lot's of code and detail in her info tab. This made me want to step up my game. For the next 48 hours, all I did was the project. I was so stressed out and tried to find new ways to make my project represent real life better while making me info tab sound smart. I had help from Mr. Holmes with some genius code, but it created unexpected bugs. I was so confused... I stared at my code for two hours trying to fix it and nothing was happening. I was so stressed out and sad. I didn't know what to do, but Mr. Holmes saved the day when he offered to take a look at my simulation. He told me that everything looked fine and to request a call from him if I was still unreasonbly stressed. I was so relieved because it was the day that the project was due. He made me realize what a simuation actually is, so I was much happier with my project and submitted it with confidence.
